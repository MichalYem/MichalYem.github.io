About Me

I am an assitant professor in the Faculty of Engineering at Bar-Ilan University, Israel. Prior to that I was an associate research scholar in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Princeton University, a postdoctoral researcher at the WSL at Stanford University and a visiting postdoctoral researcher at Princeton University. I received the PhD in electrical engineering in the joint MSc-PhD track from Bar Ilan University, Israel in 2017, and the BSc in computer engineering from the Technion - Israel Institute of Technology in 2011.

I'm looking for MSc and PhD students with strong analytical background and work ethics.
Interested students are welcome to constact me via email: yemini.michal@gmail.com, michal.yemini@biu.ac.il.

Research Interesets

My research interests include:

  • Federated learning networks

  • Resilience and control in distributed networks

  • Data processing, detection and statistical inference

  • Information theory

  • Percolation theory and its application to network connectivity
